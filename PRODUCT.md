# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

NYC-based recruiters and hiring managers actively sourcing SDE2 candidates at product companies. They're scanning multiple portfolios in one sitting, often on a laptop, during a work day. They want to quickly confirm: can this person do the job, and would I want to work with them? They're not reading every line — they're forming an impression in the first 10 seconds and deciding whether to keep scrolling.

## Product Purpose

Brock's personal portfolio. The job is to convert a cold visit into a warm reach-out: give recruiters enough confidence in Brock's skills and enough sense of his personality that the next step feels obvious. Success = an email or LinkedIn DM asking to talk.

## Brand Personality

Warm, confident, human. Like a sharp engineer who happens to be easy to talk to — not a recruiter-optimized keyword machine, not an overwrought creative portfolio. Professionally polished but never stiff. The site should feel like Brock wrote it, not like a template.

## Anti-references

- Generic Bootstrap portfolio templates (dark card grids, identical icon rows, no personality)
- Overwrought creative portfolios with full-screen animations, WebGL, cursor effects — anything that says "frontend magician" when the actual work is full-stack product engineering
- Cold corporate bios that read like a LinkedIn summary copy-pasted into HTML
- The "Hello World" + typewriter cliché (present in current hero — worth revisiting)

## Design Principles

1. **Recruit from substance, not spectacle.** The design earns trust through clarity and quality, not visual fireworks. What Brock built matters more than how the site animates.
2. **Human over polished.** Slight warmth, genuine tone, real copy. Recruiters can smell a template from a mile away; the antidote is specificity and voice.
3. **Respect the scan.** Every section should pass a 5-second skim — the name, the role, the company, the result. Details are for the people who want them, not the ones who don't.
4. **Conversion over completeness.** Every design decision should be evaluated against one question: does this make it more likely someone reaches out?
5. **Earn the scroll.** Each section should make the next one feel worth seeing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion support for all animations (site currently uses react-reveal and react-confetti). High contrast text throughout — the current dark palette is a good base but body text contrast should be verified.
